FINDINGS & CHARGING DOCUMENT

I. Statement of the Case

The Respondent, the league member known as JDBeats, is charged with a sustained scheme to defraud the NBN daily games. The conduct falls in two families: (1) consulting live player and team statistics during the Perry Game, a game premised on unaided recall; and (2) looking up the answers to run a 30-question Trivia streak, harvesting NB¥11,271 in the process. The Perry conduct is proven conclusively for two dates by token-authenticated session records and shown as a fourteen-day pattern by server logs; the Trivia conduct is proven for its single date by token-authenticated records and a coincident desktop lookup session.

III. Counts — The Perry Game

Count I — Computer-Assisted Play · June 21, 2026 · PROVEN

While his Perry session was open and authenticated as JDBeats, IP 64.185.37.35 logged 29 lookup hits from 04:20:10–04:24:07Z, opening the player pages of williamson-zion, maxey-tyrese, ingram-brandon, allen-jarrett, embiid-joel, and beal-bradley, plus team pages MIN / NOP / WAS. He submitted a first-place lineup (rank 1/5) at 05:28:54Z. Two players he had just looked up — Ingram (NOP) and Beal (WAS) — appear in that lineup; the others were viewed and rejected (comparison-shopping).

Assistance produced victories; unaided play did not.

IV. Count — The Trivia Game

Count V — Looking Up Answers During Live Play · June 19, 2026 · PROVEN

The Trivia Game tests players on NBN player statistics, drawn from the same /players/player_seasons.csv dataset the rest of the site is built on. Respondent cheated it exactly as he cheated Perry — by looking the answers up while playing — using a two-screen setup.

Between 03:23–03:46Z, while answering on his iPhone, his desktop browser made 174 stat-lookup hits — team pages (DEN, SAS, PHX, MIN, ATL, BKN, BOS…) and repeated pulls of player_seasons.csv, the exact answer key — riding an unbroken streak to 30 in a row. The desktop is affirmatively his: at 03:25:56Z it submitted a Trivia answer under his own bearer token. The reward ledger records each correct step, crediting NB¥11,271 across the run.

Concealment. Most tellingly, Respondent never submitted his score to the public Trivia leaderboard. A real 30-game run would headline that board; his name does not appear on it at all. He took the winnings while keeping the suspicious result off the visible board — conduct evidencing consciousness of guilt and intent to conceal.

V. Charges Reviewed and Not Brought

Poeltl — NO CHARGE

Respondent participated only on Jun 12 (7 guesses) and Jun 13 (5 guesses), both legitimate solves with valid guess-progression grids and no correlated lookups. An apparent "0 guesses" reading was an archive-schema artifact (the archive stores a guess count, not the guess list — a normal player showed the same) and is not treated as misconduct.

VII. Recommended Sanctions

  • Vacatur of all Perry Game results from June 8–21, 2026, and removal from those leaderboards.
  • Removal of the 30-question Trivia streak and any associated leaderboard recognition.
  • Clawback of the NB¥12,071 in ill-gotten Perry and Trivia winnings.
  • Suspension from all NBN daily games pending Committee disposition.

Counts I, II, and V are independently dispositive.
