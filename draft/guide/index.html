Draft Guide

For League Members

How the draft works

The NBN draft runs asynchronously over two days. Each pick gets a 10-minute submission window on a fixed schedule. Windows never shift — if a pick comes in late, it only affects that slot; the next team's window still runs at its scheduled time. Queue your players in advance so that when your window opens, submitting is a single click.

Round 1 Day 1 starting at noon ET · picks 1–30, one every 10 min Round 2 Day 2 starting at noon ET · picks 31–60, same cadence

Pick #1 window: 12:00–12:10 ET. Pick #2: 12:10–12:20 ET. And so on.

Watching the draft — /draft/live

The live board at /draft/live is public. No sign-in required to watch.

  • Draft Board — pick grid by round. Shows which team is on the clock, pick windows, and revealed players. Use Round 1 / Round 2 tabs (or ←→) to switch rounds.
  • Best Available — ranked pool of undrafted players with position, age, and college/country.
  • Event Log — chronological record of every pick and trade during the draft.

The status bar at the top shows who is on the clock and a live countdown. When a pick is submitted but not yet announced, you'll see "The pick is in" on that slot.

Making your pick — /draft/pick

Go to /draft/pick to manage your picks. You need to sign in with your NBN token — you'll be prompted on first visit and the token is saved in your browser. Don't have a token yet? Reach out to bryn to get one. If you see an error about your account not having a team role, contact BOD.

The queue

The queue is how you submit picks without being online at your exact window. Build it before the draft starts and keep it updated as players get taken.

1
Browse the Available Players panel on the right side of the page. Players are ranked in the consensus pool order set by BOD.
2
Click + Queue next to any player to add them to your queue. Players already in your queue show an In Queue badge instead.
3
Use ↑ ↓ to reorder your queue and ✕ to remove a player. The player at position #1 is what the Submit button sends.
4
When your pick window opens, press Submit to select your #1 queued player. Players already drafted by someone else are skipped automatically — your #2 becomes the pick if #1 was just taken.

Submitting your pick

When your window opens, a Submit → button appears on your pick slot. Click it to submit your #1 queued player. You can update your queue right up to the moment you submit. If you're online, just press Submit when you're on the clock.

Non-BOD members cannot submit before their window opens — the server enforces the window's start.

If you miss your window

The draft does not stall — the clock runs on a fixed schedule, so the next team goes on the clock at its scheduled time whether or not you've picked. If you have a player queued, BOD will submit your #1 queued player for you — so as long as your queue is stocked, you're covered even if you can't make your window. You can also submit a missed pick yourself from /draft/pick at any time. Missed picks are ordered by submission time, so whoever submits first selects first.

Bottom line: keep your queue stocked. If you're online, press Submit when you're on the clock; if you're not, BOD submits your top queued player for you. The one case to avoid is missing your window with an empty queue.

If you have multiple picks (e.g., you acquired an extra pick), each pick has its own queue window. Keep your queue stocked with more players than you have picks.

BOD only

For BOD

Before the draft

Open /draft/live and sign in. The gear icon ⚙ in the top-right corner is visible only to BOD — click it to open the Controls drawer.

Do a dry run before the real draft: walk through the full reveal sequence together. The live show writes to its own isolated picks file, so nothing you do here touches the permanent draft order or rosters. When you're done rehearsing, hit Reset to Clean Slate in the Controls drawer to clear the rehearsal picks, event log and reveals and re-seed the board from the real draft order.

Roles on draft day

Run the draft with two distinct roles. One BOD member is the presenter — they drive the reveal sequence on screen and narrate each pick. One or more other BOD members work the panel alongside them, handling everything else so the presenter never has to break flow.

Presenter

Owns the on-screen reveal: pressing Enter through the player modal, depth chart, and close steps, and pasting in highlights URLs. Should not need to touch the Controls drawer, reassign picks, or chase down a missing GM mid-show.

Panel

Watches the queue, backlog and event log in the background. Stages draft-day trades via Submit Trade in the Controls drawer (the presenter announces them on screen), submits picks on /draft/pick for a GM who's missed their window or can't be reached, and fields any other logistics — so the presenter can stay heads-down on the show. Since nothing auto-submits, the panel keeps an eye on the board for any pick marked ⚠ Awaiting pick.

The reveal sequence

Picks are submitted silently. Only BOD can reveal them — this is the "TV moment" where you announce each selection. The gear icon turns amber when at least one pick is submitted but not yet revealed.

Press Enter (with no input field focused) to begin. The sequence:

1
Player modal — shows the selected player's photo, name, position, pick badge, and highlights video (if configured). For BOD: a Highlights URL field lets you paste a YouTube embed URL right here. Paste it and click Save before pressing Enter again.
2
Depth chart — the selecting team's full roster by position, with OVR ratings and cap hold tags. Press Enter to advance.
3
Close — press Enter or Esc to dismiss. The board updates and the next pick is ready to reveal.

Clicking a team name on the Draft Board also opens their depth chart directly. Clicking a revealed player name opens their modal (useful for re-showing highlights). Player names shown in blue have a highlights video attached.

Draft-day trades

When a trade is reported mid-draft, the panel stages it with Submit Trade in the Controls drawer, and the presenter reveals it on screen with Announce Trade. This gives trades the same "TV moment" as pick reveals.

1
Stage it (panel). In Submit Trade, add one row per pick changing hands (pick → new owner) — a trade can move multiple picks. Add a free-form announcement line, then click Stage Trade. Ownership flips immediately under the hood (so the on-the-clock order and queues stay correct), but the board keeps showing the old owner and a Trade to Announce banner appears.
2
Announce it (presenter). Click Announce Trade on the banner. A modal shows your announcement text, the board updates to the new owner(s), and a Trade entry is logged.
  • Only unsubmitted picks can be traded — submitted picks are final.
  • Picks stay at the same slot number; only the owning team changes.
  • The new owner's team token is authorized to submit that pick as soon as it's staged.
  • Made a mistake? Cancel a not-yet-announced trade on the banner to revert ownership.
  • Reassign Pick is still available as a silent fix — it changes ownership immediately with no announcement (use it for corrections, not for on-air trades).
  • After the draft, each trade must be recorded as a formal transaction — see Post-draft: transactions below.

If a GM misses their window

The clock runs on a fixed schedule — the next team goes on the clock at its scheduled time regardless. Nothing auto-submits, so the missed slot shows ⚠ Awaiting pick on the board and stays open until someone submits it. The missed GM can submit from /draft/pick at any time, or a BOD member (with a privileged token) can submit on their behalf there — BOD can submit for any slot, active or missed. Missed picks are ordered by submission time, so whoever submits first selects first.

Best Available pool order

On the Best Available tab of /draft/live, BOD can drag rows to set the consensus ranking. This order is shared — GMs see the same ranking on their /draft/pick page. Update it during the draft as players are taken to keep the board current.

Rehearsing: snapshot, restore & reset

The live show reads and writes its own isolated picks file, seeded from the real draft order. Nothing you do on /draft/live — reassigns, selections, reveals — ever changes the permanent draft picks or rosters, so it's safe to rehearse freely. You just need to wipe the rehearsal data before going live. Two tools in the Controls drawer do this:

  • Save Snapshot / Restore — capture the current state, rehearse, then Restore to revert picks and state to that exact point.
  • Reset to Clean Slate — clear all picks, the event log, reveals and queues, and re-seed the board from the current permanent draft order. This is the cleanest reset before going live: it picks up any last-minute order changes, whereas a snapshot can be stale.
Do not Restore or Reset during a live draft. Both wipe the picks already made on the live board — the broadcast's picks will disappear. These are rehearsal tools only; once the real draft starts, leave them alone.

Post-draft: transactions

The live draft is isolated from permanent data — it records picks in its own feed and the event log, but does not update rosters, the permanent draft order, or transaction history. After the draft concludes, all draft selections and any draft-day trades must be recorded using the Transactions feature.

Enter draft-day trades before the pick selections. A trade only validates while the pick is still owned by the original team, so record the reassignment as a formal trade first, then record the selection (which assigns the drafted player and stamps the pick). Doing it in the other order blocks the trade.
