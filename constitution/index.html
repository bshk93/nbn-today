Article I

Foundation

Overview

The Nothing But Net Sim League (NBN) is a "NBA Fantasy Front Office" league that allows members to take total control of an NBN franchise. In the 2023 offseason, owners voted for the league to follow a future commitment to CBA rules except where exceptions were enforced by the Board of Directors, at the behest of owners.

  • The league runs on a real-time basis, and follows the NBA schedule as closely as possible.
  • Members of front offices will be responsible for drafting rookies, signing free agents, making trades, and even coaching their teams.
  • The NBN is run on NBA 2K and shows live games numerous times per week on Twitch.
  • Each day of the season, box scores will be shown for every game as they correspond to games in real life.

Mission

The principal mission of Nothing But Net, from its beginning day to its end, is to:

  • Provide a competitive environment in which basketball enthusiasts from around the world can enjoy.
  • Maintain the highest sense of ethics throughout the league in order to ensure fair play.
  • Guarantee that all participating members of the league are active and contributing to the overall growth and health of the league.
  • Reject all forms of racism and discrimination towards any party to promote a comfortable and friendly atmosphere.
Article II

Front Office Duties

An ideal NBN front office consists of an Owner, General Manager, and Head Coach. Each front office may operate in their own fashion, but here are the suggested roles of each position.

Owner

  • Set the direction and vision of the franchise (compete/tank/rebuild/etc.)
  • Hire and maintain a full staff at all times.
  • Make sure goals are always being met.

The owner is the main person in charge of the franchise and ultimately has the final say on all trades and draft choices. The owner is also allowed to replace/fire current staff members at will.

General Manager

  • Conducting trades with other teams in order to improve the roster.
  • Sign free agents during free agency.
  • Managing the salary cap.
  • Scouting future prospects entering the league.

The General Manager is the right hand man to the owner and does whatever is necessary to make sure his vision and goals are being fulfilled.

Head Coach

  • Setting the starting lineup and rotations for the team.
  • Implementing an offensive and defensive scheme best suited to the team.
  • Adjusting rotations and schemes based on the opponents' players.

Coaches can also assist in scouting future prospects for Draft Day while making recommendations on who the organization should draft and pursue during free agency.

Additional Positions

  • Assistant General Manager
  • Assistant Coach
Article III

Rules

General League Rules

  • Neither racism nor discrimination will be tolerated in the league.
  • No form of cheating is allowed in the league:
    • No tampering.
    • No real money transactions to benefit your team.
    • Using any committee privileges (if applicable) to use information/knowledge to your advantage is prohibited.
    • Use of alternate accounts is strictly prohibited.
    • Adding illegal conditions to trade agreements (e.g., committing to tank in order to heighten pick value) is prohibited.
  • Harmful sayings such as "kill yourself" are banned from the league.
  • Questioning leadership for the sole purpose of causing trouble is not allowed.
  • Punishments for improper conduct (in any form):
    • 1st Offense: 48-hour ban
    • 2nd Offense: 1-week ban
    • 3rd Offense: Permanent ban
  • Depending on the severity of the infraction, punishments will not necessarily follow this order, according to the discretion of the Board of Directors.
  • All concerns should be addressed privately with a member of the Board of Directors and/or the Commissioner.
  • You must notify your respective team and the Board of Directors if you will be inactive for a given period of time.
  • Displaying a player's personal information without his/her consent is prohibited.
  • Remember that this is a game and have fun.
  • No witch hunting.

Discord Rules

  • Listings in #tradeblock must be serious and professional.
  • Keep information channels free of chatter (#news, #leaguepolls, etc.)
  • Commands for bots should be exclusively posted in the #commands channel.
  • NSFW content belongs exclusively in the #nsfw channel.
  • No spamming of any kind, in any channel.
  • #ownerschat should be limited to serious discussion in order to encourage ideas that will help the league.
Article IV

Membership

Recruitment

Current members are encouraged to invite their respective friends in order to maximize personal experience in the league and make it more entertaining. The majority of recruitment, however, will take place on Reddit through each team's respective subreddit, as well as various internet forums and other communities. The recruitment committee will make an effort to recruit members consistently to help make the league more entertaining and maintain full staffing in each front office.

Interview Process

Those who express interest in joining the Nothing But Net Sim League will be invited to the league's Discord server. Upon entry to the Discord, the applicant will be asked to schedule a voice chat interview with a member of the Recruitment Committee. This first interview screens the candidate to make sure he/she is active, intelligent, and non-toxic to the current environment.

Once the candidate has gone through the interview process, the head of the recruitment committee shall notify the owner of the applicant's desired team, beginning the second interview. The owner is free to ask the applicant any question he/she sees fit, as long as no rules are violated.

  • Owners should be looking to fill their front offices at all times if they are managing their teams alone.

Hiring Process

As the second interview concludes, the owner of the applicant's team will notify both the applicant and the head of the recruitment committee whether or not the applicant is hired. Should the applicant be hired, the owner will announce the new hire in the #News channel. Should the applicant not be hired by his/her desired team, they remain a free agent and will have to await interviews from other interested teams.

You have a 2-week period to join a team in the NBN or contribute in another way, or you will be removed from the NBN.

Security

The Nothing But Net Sim League aspires to be an honest and fair simulation for all members. In order to promote fair play, members may be asked to disable VPNs, complete voice interviews to verify activity and legitimacy, and submit to other forms of authentication. If a member is unwilling to verify certain security measures, they may be removed from the league.

Activity Check

To combat inactivity from owners and high turnover in the league, the following methodology will be implemented:

  • Monthly #leaguepolls check-in. At the beginning of each month, a BOD member will post a poll. Polls must be responded to within 5 days of being posted (applies to all members).

Ownership strikes result from a lack of activity:

  • Failure to vote in a poll.
  • Complaints from other FO members that the owner is not around (FO members must provide proof that contact has been ignored within a 5-day span).

When one or more of the above situations occurs, the BOD will first reach out to the team FO and then the owner via DM to confirm activity. Failure to respond within 24 hours will result in a strike.

  • At 3 strikes, the owner will be asked to step down into a lesser role and will not be eligible for ownership again for one year.
  • For regular coaches and GMs who are inactive, their position will be vacated immediately.
  • Your account will be demoted to Free Agent status unless you have not been heard from within 2 weeks of being replaced. If you have left the Discord server, reach out to a BOD member via Reddit DM to be allowed back in.
  • An owner is allowed to vouch for his/her FO member before removal occurs.
Article V

Standing Committees

Recruitment Committee

It is the sole responsibility of the recruitment committee to implement and execute a strategy that effectively recruits and maintains new members in the league. The committee must also maintain a strict interview and security structure that best reflects the traits needed to flourish in the league.

Current head and members: Bryn (head), KVL, CF, That1Guy, and JD.

Free Agency Committee

The Free Agency Committee is in charge of grading each team's submitted pitch for their desired free agent. Each member of the committee (5 per FA) is given 100 percentage points to distribute between each bidding team on how likely the free agent is to play for that team. Points are then converted to balls for a lottery system.

Current members: KidMonotone, cheppywire, DarthAwn, JDDN, hkd, Jonny, Kamal, immaking, Rodney McDoom, Mega, Jaywalker7, and Wonder.

Trade Review Committee

The Trade Review Committee is responsible for reviewing all trades submitted before announcing them as completed. ALL trades must be submitted for review before they can be announced on the #transactions channel. The review board ensures that both teams are within the legal cap should the trade be approved.

  • Trade Committee must have five members at all times.
  • All trades must be analyzed to ensure compliance with the Stepien rule, cap rules, and future pick protections.
  • Three of the five TRC members must approve each trade.
  • Veto Process: Three of the five TRC members can veto a trade. This sends the trade to the BOD, where a majority (3/5) can confirm the veto or override and allow the trade.

Current members: CF (head), killerdawg7, NotChris, Adams17, and JDDN. Chuck acts as the alternative committee member if a member's team is involved in a trade or a member is away.

PO-Ext Committee

The PO-Ext committee is responsible for handling league player options in existing and future contracts and for the judgment of future contract extensions. Committee members allocate lottery balls for their decisions, using the same methodology as the FAC. Members are banned from discussing their lottery ball allocations with anyone outside the committee. Decisions will be automatically determined if the lottery ball allocation exceeds 85% in favor of any one option. The final outcomes will be streamed live by the streaming committee.

The committee will consist of a maximum of nine members with the power to make lottery ball allocations.

Rosters Committee

The Rosters Committee handles all tasks related to the maintenance of current rosters, including updating spreadsheets to reflect trades, updating cap room for each team, reflecting free agent signings, and updating each team's current picks on a regular basis.

Please allow up to 48 hours after your trade is announced for it to be reflected in the spreadsheets.

Current members: Killer, Nelson.

Streaming Committee

The streaming committee will be in charge of making sure all free agency lotteries and any type of game played with the teams are streamed live on Twitch for the rest of the league to watch. Streaming these events live guarantees that there is no form of collusion/cheating taking place.

  • The league save will be hosted on the league Shadow PC, the login to which is given only to members of the streaming committee and select members of the rosters/update committee.
  • Individual games will be streamed on a three-to-four games per week schedule, at the discretion of the committee.
  • The committee will be in charge of updating teams on the fatigue of their players.

Streaming members: Cheppy, KyleWTF, Chuck, and That1Guy. Scheduling: bjbren17.

Website and Statistics Committee

Members of the WSC assigned to the website division will be responsible for making sure everything included in the NBN Google Drive is available to the league in website format. The statistics division will consist of 4–6 members who divide the league into equal parts, with each member responsible for a certain number of teams. Stat keepers are encouraged to keep their assigned teams updated on a daily basis, and weekly at a minimum.

Current members: Bryn, Chuck.

Board of Directors

The Board of Directors is a group of seven people, appointed by a league-wide vote, who are in charge of keeping the league in order and improving all aspects of it. Each board member is usually in charge of one specific section of the league.

  • The Board of Directors votes on important changes to the league's structure overall.
  • At the end of each league season, the owners group will vote on and elect a new Board of Directors for the upcoming league season.
  • Members that served on the Board the previous year are eligible to serve multiple years in a row.
  • The Board of Directors has the power to moderate the Discord chat and delete messages.

Current members: Bryn, Not Chris, Cheppywire, Chuck, KidMonotone, Killerdawg7, and That1Guy101.

Article VI

Commissioner

Responsibilities

  • The Commissioner of the NBN Sim League will serve as the Chairman of the Board of Directors.
  • His primary responsibility will be to lead and participate in discussions among the Board of Directors.
  • If at any time during an Owner's Vote there is a tie, the Commissioner shall serve as a tiebreaker. The Commissioner will not vote on any matter unless there is a tie.

Current commissioner: Chuck.

Article VII

Amendment Procedure

Rule Changes

  • Any owner may present a rule change proposal in #ownerschat.
    • Any proposal must be accompanied by the endorsement of another owner.
  • Rule changes are typically done in the offseason.
    • Owners may still present a proposal to the Board of Directors during the season.
    • The Board of Directors will confirm whether this proposal should be voted on in-season or tabled until the offseason.

Voting

  • Any proposal will be initially voted on by the owners not serving on the Board of Directors.
  • If that vote passes, it will travel to the Board of Directors for a confirmation vote.
    • The Commissioner will serve as tiebreaker for any vote by the Board of Directors.
  • GMs and coaches have no voting power — the owner of a team is responsible for feeling out their front office's opinion.
Proposals can be submitted and tracked using the Proposals section of this site.

Article XIII

Termination, Quitting, and Penalties

Termination Process

Owners are free to fire/hire their general managers and coaches at any point during the regular season and offseason. If terminating a player from his/her team, the owner must provide a reason for the termination and submit it to the Commissioner. Players who are terminated from their previous teams are free to be interviewed and hired by another team at any point in the league's existence.

Quitting

  • Once you announce your leave, you have until the start of the new season to join a new team of your choosing. You must go through the interview process to be hired on a new team.
  • Anyone who quits during a period outside of this window must wait until the end of the season to be interviewed for another team. This prevents players from making moves on other teams and then abandoning them for another team.
  • You may not quit your team if you are the only representative for it unless you are leaving the league entirely.
Article XIV

Streaming Gameplay, Player Updates, and Rotation Updates

Streaming

  • Games throughout the regular season will be either streamed or simulated by designated committee members.
  • Games will be streamed based on the schedule set by the committee at the beginning of the season. Games will be scheduled up to the last month of the regular season before the rest are decided by league vote through polls.
  • Teams will not be allowed to rest players free of injury during a streamed game.
  • Simmed games and streamed games will be stored on the NBN YouTube channel.

Player Updates

As the season progresses, 2K will regularly update players' OVR ratings. The criteria for a player to be updated in our league (done manually) is as follows:

  • Players rated 90 OVR and above: ±1 OVR change required
  • Players rated 80–89 OVR: ±1 OVR change required
  • Players rated 79 and below: ±2 OVR change required

Before the playoffs start, any player that has changed during the season will be updated regardless of the magnitude of change.

Rotation Updates

  • All rotation updates are to occur on the team coaching spreadsheet given to each team.
  • Rotations need to be updated by 11:59 PM EST the day before your game is scheduled. You can set multiple rotations in advance.
  • Whenever you make a rotational change, please note it on the "ALL CHANGES" sheet in the Google Drive and highlight changes on the rotation sheet.
  • Teams will be allowed to fully customize their coaches' attributes and styles at allotted intervals: beginning of season, trade deadline, and end of regular season (if the team qualifies for the playoffs).
  • Teams will be informed of their players' fatigue levels as they change by members of the streaming committee.

Injuries

  • The league will be set to injury sliders of 31 to begin the season, confirmed by owners prior to the beginning of each season.
  • Injuries will be promptly reported to teams by members of the streaming committee along with expected recovery times.
  • Teams with newly injured players must update their rotation prior to the day of their next scheduled game if the injured player was initially given minutes. Failure to do so will result in an auto-sorted rotation.
Article XV

Tank Rules

  • Teams must play their best (2K overall rated) player 30+ minutes per game.
  • Teams must play their next two best players 24+ minutes per game.
  • Resting these players after back-to-backs, or very heavy weeks, for 1 game at a time is acceptable. No resting back-to-back.
  • If you break these rules, the BOD will give you a warning, and you must change your rotation to meet these requirements.
  • If you refuse to change your roster, it will be auto-sorted.
  • FOs are encouraged to inform the BOD of teams breaking these rules.
  • Constant skirting of the rules / doing the absolute minimum to comply will be tracked and documented by the BOD, and that information will be passed on to the FAC.
  • Some situations are negotiable with the BOD for circumstances like older players, players who have been injured during the season, etc. If the BOD reaches out to you, you are simply asked to fix your roster to comply with the rules that day.
  • Once eliminated from play-in contention, you are able to rest/bench/sit your players with no restrictions. Teams are still encouraged to promote realism.
